They say that your workdesk can tell the story about your life and accomplishments

What story does this desk tell?

  1. A Nintendo Switch

    2. If I’m not working, you can probably find me playing video games with friends. Video games have always been part of my life, especially Nintendo games with my first games being Pokémon. Now as an adult I still play Pokémon and have picked up Persona, Xenoblade and Fire Emblem as other franchises I love.

  2. Accessibility design

    3. I wanted to learn how to design applications and websites because I have two siblings with special needs and seeing them struggle to use certain websites that weren’t designed for their needs made me want to prevent others from struggling.

  3. Photo of my family

    4. I'm a family driven person and always prioritise family and friends, always ready to help whenever it’s needed. If I’m not playing video games or boardgames, you can find me spending quality time with family or friends.

  4. Figma

    5. After learning how to use Figma, it has become one of my favorite tools to use when designing. It has an easy learning curve with a lot of guides online and it’s also convenient for multiple designers to use for a single project. These two factors made me drop Adobe XD and move to Figma.

  5. Coding

    6. Before I decided to study UX/UI design, I was studying computer science for a year before dropping out. I have been coding since high school and still do it for fun occasionally and lets me have easier discussions with programmers regarding my designs. Currently the programming languages I know are C, C++, C#, HTML and CSS.
